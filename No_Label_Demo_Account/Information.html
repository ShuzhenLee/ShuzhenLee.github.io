You will be presented with real historical data about the performance of two funds over 45 periods. The details of the funds are as follows:

Fund A:

This fund provides exposure to a selection of large U.S. companies spanning multiple industries. It is designed for investors seeking broad market exposure to well-established firms with strong market presence. The fund aims to track the performance of a large-cap index, offering a low-cost, efficient way to invest in major companies.


Fund B:

This fund invests in a diversified portfolio of U.S. companies, aiming to achieve long-term financial performance. It provides exposure to firms across various industries while maintaining risk-adjusted returns comparable to broad market benchmarks. The fund follows a structured approach to selecting companies based on specific inclusion criteria.

Both funds are currently active in the financial market but have been anonymized for the purpose of the study. All data shown in this study represent real historical performance.
